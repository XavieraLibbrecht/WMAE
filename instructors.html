ERIK FORSYTHE

Flight Paramedic

Erik works as a Flight Paramedic in western Colorado and lives in the small but mighty ski town of Crested Butte. He has been working EMS since 1992 with experience in Mountain Rescue, Ski Patrol, Ambulance, Fire, and Flight services and has held the positions of Ski Patrol Director and EMS Chief. Erik has been teaching wilderness medicine programs since 1994 and joined the staff of Wilderness Medical AssociatesÂ® in 2000. As a Colorado EMS instructor, he has also been training EMTs since 1994. He owns and operates Professional Rescue Training, specializing in technical rope rescue education, EMS education, and Wilderness Medicine education. Outside the classroom, Erik is married and a father of two children, and still manages to honor his passions for ice and rock climbing, mountain biking, skiing, and mountaineering.

ARNE VAN BRABANT

EMT

As an outdoor person, Arne has always been attracted to the mountains. Over the last years, canyoning has become his new passion in addition to hiking, climbing and camping.
His experience in non-urban rescue and first aid stems from his job as an on- and offshore wind turbine safety instructor, where his specialty was the organization of emergency drills with regards to self-reliance in first aid and technical rescue.
Arne currently works as an EMT for the port of Antwerp and the greater Antwerp region. Besides working as an EMT, he is also studying nursing and will specialize in prehospital emergency care.

JOHAN HOVELYNCK

WFR

Johan combines work as a team trainer with specializations in outdoor training and Crew Resource Management. His passion for experiential learning and the outdoors brought him to Outward Bound early in his work life. He lead groups for several European, American and African Outward Bound Schools and acted as a safety review leader for Outward Bound International. Johan is a safety auditor for the Belgian federations for outdoor providers (BFNO) and adventure tourism (WANT) and an instructor in the state certificate training for caving and canyoneering guides (VTS).
Johan took his first WMA course when working in North Carolina in 1989 and was part of introducing WMA's curricula in Europe the following years. Keeping his focus on accident prevention during programs, he gained first-aid experience as an Emergency Medical Technician. Johan was a member of the Belgian cave rescue and rock rescue systems. He currently instructs ropes rescue for emergency services.
